2.  Introduction & Business Context

2.1  Business Background
Chertt is a chat-first conversational platform designed to help organisations run important day-to-day workflows through familiar communication channels — primarily WhatsApp, mobile app, and web. The product targets African SMEs, churches, and community-driven organisations that need operational efficiency without the complexity or cost of traditional enterprise software.
The core insight driving Chertt is that for most of its target users, WhatsApp is already the operating environment. Staff communicate, coordinate, and make decisions on WhatsApp every day. Chertt meets them there — bringing workflow automation, approvals, records, payments, and access control into the conversational interface they already use.
2.2  Business Objectives
•	Enable organisations to run structured internal workflows — approvals, requests, inventory, reporting — through WhatsApp and mobile, without adopting complex ERP systems.
•	Serve churches with purpose-built tools for member management, giving, child safety, and pastoral care that fit their unique operational context.
•	Provide small retailers and service businesses with a simple, chat-based commerce capability — catalog, orders, payments, and stock — within a 20-product scope.
•	Power event organisers with end-to-end event management from invitation through to QR-based venue access control.
•	Establish a shared-kernel platform architecture that supports rapid expansion into adjacent verticals (Schools, Gyms, Communities) without rebuilding the foundation.
•	Generate recurring subscription revenue from organisations on a per-tenant pricing model.

2.3  Target Users
User Segment	Primary Module	Key Needs
SMEs & Offices (5–200 staff)	Business Toolkit	Approvals, expense logging, inventory, facility reporting, staff directory
Churches & Faith Organisations	ChurchBase	Member management, giving, child check-in, pastoral care, first-timer follow-up
Small Retailers & Service Biz	StoreFront	Catalog, chat orders, payment links, stock alerts, delivery tracking
Event Organisers & Venues	Events	Registration, ticketing, RSVP management, QR gate access control
Admin / Platform Managers	All modules	Tenant setup, user management, reporting, configuration, billing

2.4  Scope Statement
In Scope
•	All four launch modules: Business Toolkit, ChurchBase, StoreFront, Events
•	Shared platform kernel: Auth/IAM, Workflow Engine, Document Service, Notifications Engine, Payment Adapter, QR/Code Service, File/Media Store
•	WhatsApp (WABA), mobile web (PWA), and web admin portal as delivery channels
•	Chat Mode interaction (guided, structured) across all modules
•	AI Mode (natural language, LLM-powered) as a Phase 4 capability
•	Multi-tenant architecture supporting multiple independent organisations
•	Admin portal for tenant and user management
Out of Scope (Version 1.0)
•	Native iOS and Android apps (PWA-first; native app wrapper in future phase)
•	Direct payment processing (payment links only in v1.0; direct processing pending CBN licensing)
•	Spin-off verticals: Chertt for Schools, Chertt for Registrations, Chertt for Communities
•	Voice input (AI Mode voice pipeline is a Phase 4 beta feature, not a v1.0 requirement)
•	Multi-language support beyond English (Nigerian English is the primary language target)
•	Third-party ERP or accounting system integrations (export to spreadsheet only)

2.5  Assumptions & Dependencies
Assumptions
•	WhatsApp Business API access will be obtained through an approved BSP before development commences
•	A payment gateway integration agreement (Paystack or Flutterwave) will be signed before StoreFront development begins
•	The product will be hosted on a major cloud provider (AWS or GCP) from day one
•	Pilot organisations for Business Toolkit MVP will be identified and engaged before Phase 2 development completes
•	Legal opinions on CBN payment licensing and NDPR child data obligations will be obtained before ChurchBase child check-in development
•	The platform will be operated as a multi-tenant SaaS with a subscription pricing model
Dependencies
•	Meta WhatsApp Business API — platform cannot function without WABA access
•	BSP partner — required for WABA provisioning and message template management
•	Payment gateway (Paystack or Flutterwave) — required for StoreFront and Events paid ticketing
•	LLM API (OpenAI GPT-4 or Anthropic Claude) — required for AI Mode and Smart Document drafting
•	Cloud infrastructure (AWS/GCP) — required for all hosting, storage, and CI/CD
•	Google Calendar / Microsoft Outlook API — required for Appointments feature in Business Toolkit
 
3.  Shared Platform Requirements
The following requirements apply to the entire Chertt platform across all modules. They form the shared kernel that every vertical module depends on. These must be designed, built, and stabilised before module-specific development begins.

3.1  Authentication & Identity
Every user on the Chertt platform is uniquely identified by their WhatsApp mobile phone number, supplemented by a secondary email address for document delivery and account recovery.
ID	Requirement	Description	Priority
FR-AUTH-01	Phone-Number Identity	The system shall use the user's WhatsApp phone number as their primary identity. No username/password is required.	Must Have
FR-AUTH-02	OTP Authentication	The system shall authenticate users via a One-Time Passcode (OTP) sent to their WhatsApp number at login.[KO1.1]	Must Have
FR-AUTH-03	PIN Fallback	Users may optionally set a 4–6 digit PIN as a secondary authentication method once initial OTP verification is complete.[KO2.1]	Should Have
FR-AUTH-04	Multi-Tenant Isolation	Each organisation (tenant) shall be fully isolated. Users in Tenant A cannot access data, workflows, or records belonging to Tenant B.	Must Have
FR-AUTH-05	Role-Based Access Control	Each user shall be assigned one or more roles within their organisation. Roles determine which features, data, and actions they can access.[KO3.1]	Must Have
FR-AUTH-06	Role Hierarchy	The system shall support at minimum: Super Admin, Admin, Manager, Staff, and Read-Only roles. Module-specific roles (e.g. Store Manager, Pastoral Team) shall be configurable.	Must Have
FR-AUTH-07	Authorised Signatory	Specific users may be designated as authorised signatories. Only these users can apply digital signatures to documents and approve certain workflow types.	Must Have
FR-AUTH-08	Session Management	The system shall manage user session state per WhatsApp number and per web/PWA session. Sessions shall expire after a configurable inactivity period.	Must Have
FR-AUTH-09	Audit Trail	All authentication events (login, logout, failed attempts) shall be logged with timestamp, channel (WhatsApp/web), and IP address.	Must Have
FR-AUTH-10	Secondary Email	Users may optionally register a secondary email address for document delivery and account recovery.[KO4.1]	Should Have

ID	Requirement	Description	Priority

FR-WF-02	State Machine	Each workflow instance shall progress through defined states: Draft → Submitted → Under Review → Approved / Rejected / Returned.	Must Have
FR-WF-03	Parallel & Sequential Approval	The engine shall support both sequential (one approver at a time) and parallel (all approvers simultaneously) approval modes.	Should Have
FR-WF-04	Approval Notification	Each approver shall receive a WhatsApp notification when action is required on a workflow item, including a summary of the request and action buttons.[KO5.1][KO5.2]	Must Have
FR-WF-05	Approval from WhatsApp	Approvers shall be able to approve or reject a workflow item directly from within the WhatsApp conversation without opening the web portal.	Must Have
FR-WF-06	Rejection with Comments	Approvers shall be able to reject a request and provide a reason. The requester shall be notified with the rejection reason.	Must Have
FR-WF-07	Return for Revision	Approvers shall be able to return a request to the originator for revision without rejecting it outright.	Should Have
FR-WF-08	Timeout & Escalation	If an approver does not act within a configurable time window, the system shall send a reminder and optionally escalate to the next approver or supervisor.	Should Have
FR-WF-09	Full Audit Log	Every state change in a workflow instance shall be recorded with: actor, action, timestamp, and any comments. This log shall be immutable and accessible to admins.	Must Have
FR-WF-10	Requester Status Visibility	The requester shall be able to check the current status of any workflow item they initiated at any time via WhatsApp or the web portal.	Must Have

3.7  AI / NLP Router
The AI/NLP Router enables AI Mode — where users interact with Chertt in natural language rather than guided menus. This is a Phase 4 feature but its data model and interface contracts must be designed upfront.
ID	Requirement	Description	Priority
FR-AI-01	Intent Classification	The system shall classify free-text user messages into one of the platform's defined intent categories (e.g. 'raise expense request', 'check order status', 'report facility issue').	Must Have (Ph4)
FR-AI-02	Slot Filling	Once an intent is identified, the system shall extract required data fields (slots) from the conversation, prompting the user for missing information.	Must Have (Ph4)
FR-AI-03	Context Management	The system shall maintain conversation context across multiple message turns within a session, allowing references to previously stated information.	Must Have (Ph4)
FR-AI-04	Confidence Threshold	When the AI confidence score falls below a configurable threshold, the system shall fall back to Chat Mode guidance rather than proceeding with uncertain interpretation.	Must Have (Ph4)
FR-AI-05	Chat Mode Fallback	Chat Mode shall always be available as a fallback for any user or any interaction, regardless of AI Mode availability.	Must Have
FR-AI-06	LLM Document Drafting	The Document Service shall use an LLM API call to generate document drafts from user-provided plain language descriptions. This is distinct from the AI Mode intent classifier.	Must Have

7.  Module: Events
Chertt Events is the most self-contained module. It handles end-to-end event management from invitation through to QR-based venue access control. The QR check-in[KO32.1] system is the technical differentiator and must work reliably under crowd conditions, including offline-capable operation.
7.1  Module Overview

Target Users	Event organisers, venue managers, ushers, registrants, ticketed guests
Primary Channel	WhatsApp (guest-facing), web admin portal (organiser-facing), PWA (gate usher)
Key Dependency	Payment Adapter (paid tickets), QR/Code Service, Notifications Engine
Build Phase	Phase 3 — Months 6–9 (parallel with StoreFront)

8.  Non-Functional Requirements
Non-functional requirements (NFRs) apply across all modules and define the quality, performance, security, and compliance standards the platform must meet. These are not optional and must be incorporated into the architecture and development standards from day one.
8.1  Performance
Category	Requirement	Target Metric
API Response Time	All API responses for non-AI operations shall be returned within the target time under normal load	< 500ms at p95
AI Mode Latency	AI Mode intent classification responses shall complete within the target time	< 3 seconds at p95
QR Check-in Latency	QR scan to pass/fail result shall be returned within the target time in online mode	< 800ms
Concurrent Users	Platform shall support concurrent active users per tenant without degradation	500 concurrent users per tenant
WhatsApp Message Delivery	Outbound WhatsApp notifications shall be dispatched within the target time of triggering event	< 30 seconds
Document Generation	PDF generation (letterhead, invoice, receipt) shall complete within the target time	< 10 seconds
Uptime SLA	Platform availability shall meet the target monthly uptime excluding planned maintenance windows	99.5% monthly uptime

8.2  Security
Category	Requirement	Standard / Target
Encryption at Rest	All data at rest — database, object storage, backups — shall be encrypted	AES-256
Encryption in Transit	All data in transit between clients and servers and between services shall be encrypted	TLS 1.2 minimum; TLS 1.3 preferred
Tenant Data Isolation	Row-level security shall be enforced at the database layer. Application-layer tenant_id filtering alone is insufficient.	PostgreSQL RLS enforced
OWASP Top 10	All web interfaces and APIs shall be designed and tested against the OWASP Top 10 vulnerability categories	OWASP Top 10 compliance
API Authentication	All API endpoints shall require valid authentication tokens. No unauthenticated endpoints shall exist except OTP initiation.	JWT / OAuth 2.0
Penetration Testing	An independent penetration test shall be conducted before any public launch	Annual pen test minimum
Secrets Management	API keys, credentials, and secrets shall not be stored in code repositories. A secrets management service shall be used.	AWS Secrets Manager or equivalent

8.3  Compliance
Regulation	Requirement	Action Required
NDPR (Nigeria)	All personal data collected, stored, and processed shall comply with the Nigeria Data Protection Regulation. A privacy notice and consent mechanism shall be presented to all users.	Privacy notice, consent records, DPO appointment
WhatsApp / Meta ToS	All WABA usage shall comply with Meta's WhatsApp Business Policy and Messaging Policy. Template messages shall be submitted for approval.	Template pre-approval, usage policy adherence
CBN Regulations	Payment processing shall comply with Central Bank of Nigeria regulations. v1.0 uses payment links only. Direct processing requires separate licensing.	Legal opinion; no direct processing in v1.0
Data Residency	For Nigerian users, preference shall be given to data residency within Nigeria or a country with equivalent data protection standards.	Cloud region selection (AWS Lagos preferred)
Child Data	Processing of children's data requires explicit parental consent and heightened protection under NDPR. A safeguarding policy shall be documented.	Legal review before ChurchBase child check-in build

8.4  Scalability & Reliability
•	The platform shall be designed to scale horizontally — adding capacity through additional server instances rather than requiring larger single servers.
•	Database connection pooling shall be implemented to support high concurrency without connection exhaustion.
•	The WhatsApp message dispatch queue shall be designed to handle bursts (e.g. bulk event invitations) without blocking other system operations.
•	Automated database backups shall run daily, with point-in-time recovery capability to within 1 hour of any data loss event.
•	All critical services shall have health check endpoints monitored by an automated alerting system (e.g. PagerDuty or equivalent).
•	Planned maintenance windows shall be communicated to tenant admins at least 48 hours in advance.

8.5  Usability
•	All WhatsApp interactions shall be designed to complete any end-to-end task in no more than 7 message turns.
•	All guided (Chat Mode) flows shall be testable by a non-technical user with no training in under 5 minutes.
•	Error messages across all channels (WhatsApp, web, PWA) shall be plain-language and action-oriented, not technical.
•	The admin web portal shall be accessible on Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge (latest two major versions of each).
•	The QR check-in PWA shall function on any Android or iOS smartphone with a camera, without requiring app installation.
 
9.  Acceptance Criteria
The following acceptance criteria define the minimum conditions for each module to be considered ready for production launch. All Must Have functional requirements for the relevant module must be implemented and pass user acceptance testing (UAT) before a module may be released.
9.1  Platform Foundation Acceptance
•	Multi-tenant data isolation is verified: data from Tenant A cannot be accessed by Tenant B users under any test condition
•	WhatsApp OTP authentication completes successfully for a new user registration in under 60 seconds
•	A workflow with 3-level approval chain completes correctly: submission → approval 1 → approval 2 → approval 3 → completion, with full audit log
•	A rejection at any approval level correctly notifies the requester with the rejection reason within 60 seconds
•	Escalation fires correctly when an approver does not act within the configured timeout window
•	All system notifications are delivered to the correct WhatsApp recipient(s) within 30 seconds of the triggering event
•	Payment link generation and webhook-based payment confirmation function end-to-end with the configured PSP in the production environment

9.2  Business Toolkit Acceptance
•	A staff member can initiate an AI-drafted letter, route it for approval, have it signed by an authorised signatory, and receive the signed PDF on WhatsApp — all without leaving WhatsApp
•	An invoice created by a staff member and approved by a manager is correctly formatted on the organisation's invoice template with accurate totals
•	A supply request is submitted, routed to the store manager, stock is verified, and release is approved — with all parties notified at each step via WhatsApp
•	An issue report with a photo attachment is submitted, routed to the facilities team, and status updates are sent to the reporter at each stage
•	An expense log with receipt photo is submitted and exported correctly to an XLSX file with all required fields
•	A custom form created in the admin portal is distributed via WhatsApp and responses are captured and exportable to XLSX
•	The staff directory is searchable by name and department, and click-to-chat initiates a WhatsApp conversation correctly

9.3  ChurchBase Acceptance
•	A child profile is created with parental consent recorded. The child's QR code is generated and functional at check-in
•	Child check-in and check-out correctly notifies the registered parent via WhatsApp with timestamp
•	A giving transaction is initiated via WhatsApp, a payment link is generated, payment is confirmed, and a giving receipt is delivered to the member
•	A first-timer registration triggers the automated welcome message within 60 seconds and the follow-up sequence activates correctly
•	A prayer request submitted with anonymity toggled on does not reveal the requester's identity to the pastoral team
•	A pastoral care request is visible only to designated pastoral team members and not to general admin users
•	Child data is confirmed to be accessible only by users with explicit child data permissions — verified by attempting access with a non-authorised account

9.4  StoreFront Acceptance
•	A customer can browse the catalog, place an order for multiple products, receive a payment link, complete payment, and receive an invoice and receipt — entirely via WhatsApp
•	Stock levels correctly decrement on order confirmation and the out-of-stock block prevents ordering when stock reaches zero
•	A low-stock alert is sent to the merchant when product quantity falls to the configured threshold
•	A payment link that is not paid within the expiry window correctly expires, and the customer is notified
•	An order status update (e.g. Dispatched) is received by the customer via WhatsApp within 60 seconds of the merchant updating the status
•	The stock report and order history are accurate and match the recorded transactions in the system

9.5  Events Acceptance
•	A guest can register for a paid event via WhatsApp, receive a payment link, complete payment, and receive their QR-coded ticket — all without leaving WhatsApp
•	A QR code scan at a venue check-in point returns a pass/fail result in under 800ms in online mode
•	A QR code that has already been scanned returns an 'Already Checked In' result and denies re-entry
•	The offline-capable check-in mode functions correctly without internet connectivity and syncs correctly on reconnection
•	Two ushers scanning at simultaneous entry points do not create duplicate check-in records for the same ticket
•	The post-event attendance report accurately reflects all check-in records
•	Guest-specific RSVP details (table number, parking) are sent only to the correct individual guest

Appendix C — Open Questions
The following questions require resolution by the product owner or legal counsel before or during development:
Q1 — WABA BSP Selection
Which BSP (360dialog, Infobip, Twilio) will be used for WhatsApp Business API access? This determines onboarding timeline and per-message pricing.
Q2 — PSP Selection
Will Paystack or Flutterwave be the primary payment service provider? This affects settlement timelines, fee structure, and integration effort.
Q3 — CBN Licensing
Has legal counsel confirmed the regulatory pathway for eventual direct payment processing? This determines the StoreFront roadmap beyond v1.0.
Q4 — Child Data Legal Framework
Has a NDPR-compliant child data processing policy been prepared and reviewed by a data protection officer? This is a blocker for ChurchBase child check-in development.
Q5 — Cloud Region
Will AWS Lagos (af-south-1) or another region be used? This affects data residency compliance and latency for Nigerian users.
Q6 — Tenant Pricing Model
What is the subscription pricing model per tenant, per module? This affects tenant onboarding flow design and billing integration scope.
Q7 — Pilot Organisations
Which 2–3 organisations have been identified as Business Toolkit MVP pilot partners? Pilot selection should influence feature prioritisation within Must Have scope.
