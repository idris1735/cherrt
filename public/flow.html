How it actually works

No app to install. No training session. Just the WhatsApp your members already use every day. For pastors and admins — a clean dashboard when you need it.

WhatsApp is the front door

Members text Chertt like they'd text anyone. They give, register, ask for prayer, check in their kids — all from the same chat. No menus. No confusion.

A dashboard that stays out of the way

Pastors, finance, and admins get a simple web view — reports, approvals, member records. You only open it when you need it.

AI that understands what's serious

Chertt knows the difference between "pray for my mum" and "I don't want to be here anymore." The first goes to the prayer team. The second triggers an instant alert to a named leader. No exceptions.

Built for real WhatsApp behaviour

Voicenotes get transcribed. Receipt photos get read — numbers pulled out automatically. Someone disappears mid‑conversation and comes back next week? Chertt remembers exactly where you left off.

Getting started — five minutes, not five days

This isn't enterprise software. There's no implementation team. A pastor can have Chertt running for their entire church before the next service.

1

Pastor or admin creates the workspace

Opens the Chertt dashboard on their phone or laptop. Enters the church name, city, and timezone. Picks which modules to activate — Toolkit, ChurchBase, StoreFront, Events. Two minutes.

2

Links the church WhatsApp number

Scans a QR code from the dashboard. That number becomes Chertt. The same number members already have saved. No new number to remember.

3

Invites members — in bulk, or one at a time

Uploads a contact list, or sends an invite link to the church WhatsApp group. Members click one link and they're in. Chertt already knows their name and role.

4

Members just say Hi

That's it. No training. No app to install. A member opens WhatsApp, messages the church number, and Chertt responds — ready to give, pray, register, or check in.

First time — a member says hello
Sister Amara: Hi

Chertt: Good afternoon, Sister Amara 👋 I'm Chertt — your church assistant. Need to give? Pray? Register for something? Just ask. I'm here.

Pastor's dashboard — same moment
Sister Amara just connected. 247 members active this week. 34 first‑timers. 12 prayer requests. ₦1.8M in giving. All from WhatsApp. All in real time.

It doesn't forget people

Most systems lose track of a member the moment they walk out the door. Chertt follows up on its own — nobody has to remember to check in.

Prayer follow‑up

"You asked us to pray for your mum last month — how is she doing now?"

Missed Sundays

"We haven't seen you the last three weeks. Everything okay? Need anything?"

Counselling check‑in

"You requested counselling two weeks ago. Did you get to meet with Pastor John?"

First‑timer nurture

Day 1: "Thanks for visiting." Day 3: a note from the pastor. Day 7: an invitation back. Automatic — but it never sounds automatic.

Where it stops being clever

"Pray for my mother" and "I don't want to be here anymore" cannot go through the same pipe. The moment Chertt picks up a signal of real danger, it stops trying to help — and starts an alarm.

Hard rule — never broken

  • The AI never attempts to counsel a crisis itself. Ever.
  • It escalates instantly — to a named leader, not a general inbox.
  • Every escalation is logged. Nothing is silently lost.
  • Location can be shared, with the person's consent.

Built for how people actually use WhatsApp

A menu‑driven bot breaks the moment someone goes off‑script. Chertt was designed for the messiness of real conversation.

People don't follow menus"My son has malaria and I need the pastor" has to work exactly like picking option 3. That takes real language understanding, not a numbered list.
Voice notesTranscribed automatically before Chertt reads them. Someone can speak their request and get the same result as typing it.
PhotosRead, not just stored. Send a picture of a receipt — Chertt pulls the numbers out and logs the expense.
PDFsParsed for the data inside them. Upload a document and Chertt extracts what matters.
People vanish mid‑conversationThey come back next week — Chertt remembers exactly where they left off. No starting over.
Shared phoneHusband and wife, same WhatsApp. Chertt figures out who's speaking and keeps their identities separate.
One person, several churchesSwitching between workspaces happens automatically. The member never has to think about it.
Member changes numberTheir identity carries over. It's not lost with the old SIM card.
Deleted WhatsAppSession recovers cleanly on reinstall. No data lost.
Pastor changesPermissions transfer as a config update — not a support ticket.
